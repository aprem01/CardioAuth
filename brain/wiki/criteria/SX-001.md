# SX-001 — New or worsening symptoms since baseline

**Category:** SX · **Severity:** required · **Pathway group:** `independent`

Applies to CPT 78492, 78491, 78452, 78451, 75557, 75574, 93306, 93350.

## Definition
Chart documents a clear change in symptoms (new onset, worsening severity, change in character, or change in exertional threshold) compared to baseline or prior study. Critical for repeat or follow-up imaging.

## Required elements
- **symptom_documented** — A specific symptom (angina, dyspnea, syncope, etc.) is named
- **change_vs_baseline** — Explicit statement of change vs. prior state — "new", "worsening", "progressed", "increased frequency". "No new complaints" or single fleeting episode without comparison does NOT satisfy.

## Validation history

- **2026-04-13** (Peter C2 / C4 / C5) — Symptoms documented but NO comparison to baseline. Single fleeting episodes in C2; explicit "no new complaints" in C5. System was marking SX-001 met on symptom presence alone. Drove `change_vs_baseline` required_element.

## Notes

SX-001 is one of the most-often-missed criteria because clinicians
frequently document symptoms without comparing to prior state. This
is a documentation-habit problem more than a clinical one — the
information exists in the physician's head but doesn't make it into
the note.
